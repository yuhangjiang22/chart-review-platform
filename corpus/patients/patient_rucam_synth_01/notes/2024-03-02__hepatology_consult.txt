HEPATOLOGY CONSULT — 2024-03-02

62-year-old patient referred for acute hepatocellular liver injury. Started
amoxicillin/clavulanate on 2024-02-20 for sinusitis; presented 2024-03-01 with
malaise and jaundice. Drug was stopped on 2024-03-01.

LABS: ALT 620 U/L (ULN 52), ALP 130 U/L (ULN 125) on 2024-03-01 — R ratio ~11,
hepatocellular pattern. Baseline ALT 30 (2024-02-20). By 2024-03-09 ALT had
fallen to 250 (>50% from peak), and to 70 by 2024-03-31.

WORKUP: viral hepatitis serologies negative (HAV IgM, HBsAg, HCV Ab); CMV/EBV
negative. No biliary obstruction on imaging. No alcohol use disorder, no
hypotension/ischemia, no autoimmune features. Concomitant lisinopril (chronic,
not hepatotoxic).

ASSESSMENT: Probable amoxicillin/clavulanate-induced hepatocellular DILI;
improving after drug cessation.
