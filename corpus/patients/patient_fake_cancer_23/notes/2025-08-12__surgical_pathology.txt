SURGICAL PATHOLOGY REPORT
Specimen: Left upper lobe lung mass, lobectomy
Specimen received: 2025-08-08
Report finalized: 2025-08-12
Pathologist: R. Alvarez, MD

GROSS DESCRIPTION
Lobectomy specimen containing a 3.4 cm firm, tan-white mass abutting but
not invading the pleura. Margins grossly clear.

MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION
The tumor is biphasic. Approximately 60% of the tumor volume shows
malignant glandular architecture with mucin production, consistent with
adenocarcinoma. The remaining ~40% shows keratinizing nests with
intercellular bridges, consistent with squamous cell carcinoma. The two
components are intimately admixed. Each component comprises well over the
10% threshold required for a combined diagnosis.

IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY
- TTF-1: positive in the glandular component, negative in the squamous component
- Napsin A: positive in the glandular component
- p40: positive in the squamous component, negative in the glandular component
- CK5/6: positive in the squamous component

FINAL DIAGNOSIS
Left upper lobe, lobectomy: ADENOSQUAMOUS CARCINOMA of the lung
(adenocarcinoma component ~60%, squamous cell carcinoma component ~40%).
Pleural margin negative. No lymphovascular invasion identified.

R. Alvarez, MD — electronically signed 2025-08-12
